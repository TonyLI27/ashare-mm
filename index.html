4  A50 overnight overlay (long YINN / FXI)

What this is. An overnight LONG of US-listed China proxies (YINN = 3× FTSE China 50, FXI = China large-cap, both USD) overlaid on the A-share market-making portfolio, to capture the move in Chinese equities while the A-share market is closed.

Exact buy / sell times used. Buy = the first 1-min bar after the A-share 15:00 close, which is ≈ Beijing 16:00 (ET 04:00, US pre-market open). Sell = the last 1-min bar before the A-share 09:30 open, which is ≈ Beijing 08:00 (ET 20:00, US after-hours close). The trade is booked on the A-share open (cover) day so it lines up with the overnight-short leg of the portfolio.

Why it cannot sit exactly on the China close/open. YINN and FXI trade on US venues, and this data feed (Databento XNAS.ITCH, Nasdaq) only covers ET 04:00–20:00 = Beijing 16:00 → 08:00 next day. The A-share close (15:00) and open (09:30) Beijing fall in the gap when these Nasdaq sessions are not trading, so we use the closest available prints — about 1 h after the China close and ~1.5 h before the China open.

To match the China close/open exactly you need the US overnight session (ET 20:00–04:00 = Beijing 08:00–16:00), which is where 15:00 and 09:30 Beijing live. That trades on Blue Ocean ATS (and the 24X National Exchange) — subscribe to that overnight feed (for example the Databento Blue Ocean dataset) for 1-min / tick bars, and the buy/sell could be pinned to 15:00:00 and 09:30:00 Beijing.

Notional per night. $885,000 each night (= the total daily deployed notional of the portfolio: 6 stocks × $77.5k + 3 ETFs × $140k). Fee: 5 bps per leg, charged on both the buy and the sell. PnL = notional × (sell / buy − 1) − fees. Prices are USD, so no FX conversion.

5  Correlation-weighted FXI hedge (dynamic, FXI only)

Idea. Instead of Section 4's flat $885k FXI every night, size the FXI buy by how each asset's overnight return co-moves with FXI's overnight return, using a 21-trading-day rolling correlation that updates daily.

Returns (China-time, same buy/sell points as Section 4). Asset overnight return ri(T) = openi(T) / closei(T−1) − 1. FXI overnight return rFXI(T) = FXI(sell ≈ Beijing 08:00, T) / FXI(buy ≈ Beijing 16:00, T−1) − 1.

Sizing & no look-ahead. The FXI bought at 16:00 on day T−1 uses the rolling correlation over returns through day T−1 (both ri and rFXI for T−1 are known by then). FXI notional that night = F(T) = Σi ρi × Ni (raw signed ρ, so a negatively-correlated asset contributes a short FXI amount; Ni = that asset's deployed notional). All 9 assets included. PnL = F(T) × rFXI(T) − 5 bps/leg.

Warm-up. The first 21 trading days have too few observations for a correlation, so the hedge = 0 there (grey band in the All view; the 6-month view is already warmed up from 2025 data).
